International Entertainment Marketing Materials

01 Overview

The client, a division of the world's leading live entertainment company, is a full-service creative agency specializing in audio-visual production, interactive design and development, graphic design, digital media, and marketing initiatives.

Problems

  • The legacy e-commerce platform was built on Magento 1, which was approaching end-of-life support. This created significant risks in security, performance, and scalability—necessitating a full migration to ensure long-term stability and support.
  • The visual design and user experience were outdated and not optimized for responsiveness or scalability. The interface failed to adapt effectively across devices and no longer reflected the brand’s evolution, resulting in a fragmented and inconsistent experience.

Project Goals

  • Migrate from Magento 1 to Magento 2 to establish a secure, stable foundation that ensures long-term scalability, performance improvements, and compatibility with evolving technologies.
  • Enhance performance and flexibility by replacing the default Magento theme with a custom React front end integrated with the Magento 2 API—creating a faster, more adaptable, and maintainable architecture.
  • Optimize the user journey to increase registrations, simplify ordering, and reduce abandonment through clear information architecture and guided workflows.
  • Redesign the interface with a modern, responsive system that reflects the brand’s evolution and adheres to contemporary UX standards, while maintaining familiar workflows for returning users.

Role

  • Discovery – Conduct stakeholder interviews, define user groups, map existing workflows, and perform competitive and platform research to identify usability issues and inform user-centered solutions.
  • Strategy – Develop detailed user profiles, restructure information architecture, map end-to-end journeys, and translate insights into clear goals that guide design and development.
  • Design – Iterate on sketches, create high-fidelity wireframes, and build interactive prototypes to deliver a responsive, inclusive, and visually modern experience aligned with brand and user needs.
  • Development – Build static, styled templates for React and Magento implementation, integrate accessibility best practices, and review builds to ensure consistency, usability, and a cohesive user experience.

Challenges

  • Designing an experience that balanced the diverse needs of multiple user groups, including venues and promoters seeking marketing services, and labels, tour managers, and artists producing materials—each with distinct goals and priorities.
  • Visually translating a highly complex ordering system—featuring print and digital marketing options, customizable packages, and artist-specific variations—into a streamlined, guided experience that optimized efficiency, accuracy, and usability.
  • Maintaining project momentum amid the disruptions of the COVID-19 pandemic, adapting processes and timelines to mitigate delays in development and deployment while navigating an industry heavily impacted by event cancellations.
Taking on a complex ordering platform with countless product variations, I reimagined the experience as a clear, guided workflow—simplifying decisions without sacrificing precision or control.

Working with a design-savvy client, my design role began modestly. Familiar with their brand, I confidently refined the high-fidelity wireframes with color and imagery that ultimately defined the site’s design direction.

Discovery

I began by formulating key questions and conducting stakeholder interviews to establish objectives, clarify user expectations, and uncover technical constraints. From there, I defined primary user groups and their needs to guide subsequent research and design decisions. I then mapped the existing order and checkout workflows to identify usability issues and bottlenecks within the current system. Competitive research helped evaluate industry standards and best practices, providing context for improvement opportunities. Finally, I performed an in-depth review of Magento 2’s architecture and capabilities to ensure all proposed solutions were both feasible and optimized for the platform.

Strategy

Building on insights from discovery, I created detailed user profiles that reflected the diverse needs of the platform’s audiences—from onboarding existing users to attracting new ones. With these personas established, I restructured the information architecture and mapped end-to-end user journeys, ensuring clarity and continuity from the homepage through order confirmation. Guided by stakeholder input and user insights, I refined the project goals to form a strategic foundation for all design and development decisions.

Design

Following strategy, I rapidly iterated on sketches to establish foundational structure, which informed the development of detailed, high-fidelity wireframes. The design focused on creating an inclusive, responsive experience that addressed all user groups, improved upon the legacy application visually, and aligned with modern trends while remaining consistent with brand guidelines. To support iteration and stakeholder alignment, I developed interactive prototypes that validated design decisions and ensured coherence with project goals and user needs.

Development

I developed static, styled templates for all screens and delivered them to the React and Magento development teams for implementation. Throughout this process, I proactively integrated accessibility considerations to address usability challenges and ensure compliance with best practices. Finally, I reviewed and refined the React implementations to ensure consistency in design, usability, and accessibility across the application—delivering a cohesive, intuitive user experience.

03 Results

Solution

A subtle modernization of the original design that unified strategy and met the needs of all user groups.

Redefining the First Impression

Introducing new users to the brand’s artist roster while easing existing users’ transition to a modern interface.

The redesigned homepage introduced a sleek, immersive “Tour” that spotlighted the client’s most prominent artists and showcased their diverse design work—from print to broadcast—while strategically maintaining legacy button placements to guide returning users seamlessly into a refreshed, more intuitive experience.

Designing for Every Device

Creating a thoughtful mobile fallback to enhance accessibility and first impressions.

While research showed limited mobile use due to the complexity of the ordering process, a responsive fallback experience was introduced to engage new users and ensure a polished, consistent first impression—offering mobile-friendly formatting and functionality without compromising the platform’s intended objectives.

Streamlining Complexity Through Guided Design

Simplifying a multifaceted ordering system while supporting scalability and flexibility.

A guided ordering experience was introduced to help users navigate a highly complex system with extensive customization options, supported by a scalable architecture capable of handling intricate programmatic logic. The process culminated in a familiar yet tailored single-page checkout that offered multiple payment options and a seamless path to completion.

Custom Experience, Built for Familiarity

Bridging brand identity with usability through seamless backend integration.

An entirely custom user admin was developed to integrate directly with the Magento backend, offering a branded experience that felt fresh yet familiar to existing users—maintaining continuity while elevating functionality and design consistency.
